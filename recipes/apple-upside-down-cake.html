Apple Upside Down Cake

"Is it a pie or a cake? Who knows?"

A delicious dessert for fall or winter that combines a cinnamon-spiced cake with buttery caramelized apples. Essentially two desserts in one!

Ingredients

    Cake
  • 1 and 1/2 cups all purpose flour
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (softened to room temp)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed light or dark brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs (at room temp)
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 6 tablespoons while milk (at room temp)
    Topping
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup packed light or dark brown sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 2 medium apples (peeled and sliced into 1/4 inch slices)

Instructions

    Preheat oven to 350 degress F or 177 degrees C
    Preparing the topping
    1. Combine butter and brown sugar together in small sauce pan over medium heat
    2. Whisk occasionally until butter has melted
    3. Cook, whisking constantly, for 1 minute as mixture thickens
    4. Remove from heat and whisk in the cinnamon and vanilla extract
    5. Pour into an ungreased 9x2 inch pie dish or round cake pan (make sure the dish is atleast 2 inches deep)
    6. Arrange the apple slices neatly on top, overlapping where necessary
    7. Place pan in the refridgerator for a few minutes as you prepare the cake batter
    Making the cake
    1. Whisk the flour, baking powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt together
    2. Set this aside
    3. Using a handheld or stand mixer with a paddle/whisk attachment, beat the butter on high speed until smooth and creamy
    4. Add both sugars and beat on high speed until creamed together
    5. Scrap down the sides and up the bottom of the bowl with a rubber spatula as needed
    6. On high speed, beat in the eggs and vanilla extract until combined
    7. Scrape down the sides and up the bottom of the bowl as needed
    8. Pour the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients
    9. Turn the mixer onto low speed and as the mixer runs, slowly pour in the milk
    10. Beat on low speed just until all of the ingredients are combined
    11. Do not over-mix (You may need to whisk it all by hand to make sure there are no lumps)
    12. Remove topping from the refridgerator
    13. Pour and spread cake batter evenly over topping
    14. Bake for 40-46 minutes, tenting foil on top of the cake halfway through bake time to prevent the top from over-browning
    15. The cake is done when a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out mostly clean
    16. Remove cake from the oven and cool on a wire rack for just 15 mintues
    17. Invert the slightly cooled cake onto a cake stand or serving plate
    18. Some of the juices from the topping will seep over the sides - that's ok
    19. You can slice and serve the cake warm, but the slices will be messy
    20. Ideally, letting the cake fully cool gives the best results
    21. Do not refridgerate the cake to speed up this process
    22. Cover leftover slices and store for up to 3 days in refridgerator or 3 months in the freezer
